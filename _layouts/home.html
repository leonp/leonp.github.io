A potted history of managing websites

I’ve managed not for profit organisations’ websites since 2008. When I started it was all about commissioning a new, trendy site: now it’s about researching customers, testing and coming up with a strategy that makes sense of it all. At least it should be – there are still plenty of pretty but hard to use websites out there.

Not for profit websites are usually an inward looking, tangled mess of too much, impenetrable stuff. I find out what customers want to do and make those tasks as easy as possible. Sounds simple, but it’s surprisingly difficult to view what you publish from your customers’ point of view. In fact it’s often downright counter–intuitive and misunderstood. Us web managers have to be able to explain difficult concepts. We also have thick skins.

Building web things since 2008

Back in 2008 (it was a good year!) Smashing Magazine linked to a WordPress theme I'd made available on my site. As well as knocking over my server this gave me some excellent publicity – people started asking me to build their websites.

Then came design for smartphones, and I can claim to have published the very first mobile first theme to the WordPress theme directory. I've been building mobile first websites for small organisations since 2010 – I've saved several organisations the hassle (and cost!) of rebuilding their websites for mobiles and tablets.

I look at what your customers want to do online, your requirements and budget and come up with a website that’ll get your business working online.
